How much does it cost to register a company in Kenya?

For a standard Private Limited Company, the baseline government incorporation fee is commonly listed as KES 10,650.

Quick rule: Use the live eCitizen/BRS invoice as the final payable amount. Depending on the filing details, the invoice may differ from a general fee reference shown elsewhere.

Why do some sources show KES 10,650 and others KES 10,750?

Official BRS materials do not always display the same figure in every place. The general Companies Registry fee schedule lists the private limited company filing at KES 10,650, while a BRS FAQ guide for the private limited filing flow mentions KES 10,750. The safest approach is simple: pay the final amount shown on your live eCitizen/BRS invoice.

Practical takeaway: Use general fee schedules for budgeting, but use the actual portal invoice for payment.

What is included in the government fee?

For a standard private limited company, the baseline fee mainly covers the incorporation filing, registry processing, and issuance of the certificate of incorporation once the application is approved.

  • Private limited company incorporation filing on the BRS platform
  • Registrar review and processing of the application
  • Certificate of incorporation after approval

It does not automatically cover later compliance work, business permits, tax setup, bank-document support, or sector-specific approvals.

How payment works on eCitizen/BRS

  1. Log in to eCitizen and select Business Registration Service.
  2. Complete the filing steps for the chosen entity type.
  3. Review the generated invoice before making payment.
  4. Pay using the options displayed in the portal and keep proof of payment.

Other costs after incorporation

The incorporation fee is only one part of the wider setup budget. After your company is registered, the next costs depend on what the business will do, where it will operate, and whether it needs extra compliance support.

Budgeting tip: Split your budget into (1) incorporation fee, (2) immediate post-incorporation compliance, and (3) operating licences / sector approvals.
  • Company search or registry extracts for bank KYC, tenders, due diligence, or shareholder confirmation
  • KRA PIN / tax setup on iTax depending on business activities and tax obligations
  • County business permit before trading
  • Sector licences or regulatory approvals for regulated activities
  • Bank account opening support documents and compliance packs
  • Professional support fees for legal, secretarial, accounting, nominee, or registered office services
  • Post-registration changes such as directors, address, company name, or secretary details

In practice, many businesses treat incorporation as the first government cost, not the last one.

  • Directors’ or shareholders’ IDs or passports
  • KRA PIN details where applicable
  • Proposed company names for search and reservation

  • Beneficial ownership details where required

What can change the final amount?

  1. Entity type — private limited, foreign company, LLP, public company, or company limited by guarantee
  2. Share capital — filing variables related to capital can affect the final amount
  3. Portal-generated invoice details — the live invoice should always override rough budgeting figures
  4. Professional support — agency or legal assistance is separate from government filing fees
  5. Post-incorporation needs — permits, bank onboarding, and extra compliance can expand the real setup budget

FAQs: Company Registration Cost in Kenya

These FAQs focus on fees and invoice planning. For the full filing process, see our company registration guide.

How much does it cost to register a company in Kenya?

For many private limited companies, the baseline government fee is commonly listed as KES 10,650. Always confirm the final payable amount on your eCitizen/BRS invoice before payment.

Why do some sources show KES 10,650 and others KES 10,750?

Different official BRS materials may display different figures for the same filing flow. The safest approach is to rely on the live portal invoice generated for your application before making payment.

Does share capital change registration fees?

Yes. Share capital and related filing details can affect the total amount shown on the portal invoice.

Are professional fees included in the government registration fee?

No. Government filing fees are separate from professional support fees, post-incorporation compliance costs, and sector-specific approvals.
